Dear Editor,

I am submitting the manuscript titled **"FraudBERT: LLM-Enhanced Feature Encoding for Insurance Fraud Detection"** for consideration for publication in *Expert Systems with Applications*.

**Summary of Contribution:**
This paper addresses a key open problem in insurance fraud detection: the effective encoding of high-cardinality categorical claim features (vehicle category, incident type, insured occupation, policy type) which carry strong fraud signals but are poorly handled by standard one-hot or ordinal encoders. We propose FraudBERT, a lightweight framework leveraging pre-trained sentence transformer embeddings to represent categorical features semantically, fused with numerical features and fed into ensemble and neural classifiers.

**Why this is novel:**
No prior work has systematically studied LLM-based categorical feature encoding specifically for insurance fraud detection. We fill this gap with experiments on two public datasets (Vehicle Claim Fraud Detection; Insurance Claims Fraud), nine baseline and proposed model comparisons, and ablation studies on embedding dimensionality and imbalance strategies.

**Suitability for this journal:**
This work falls squarely within the journal's scope of applied AI and expert systems. The practical deployment discussion, public datasets, and reproducible experiments make it directly useful to practitioners in the insurance industry.

All code and experimental configurations will be made publicly available upon acceptance.
